# Glossary — methodology terms in this report

Plain-language definitions for readers new to Ivan Zamesin's AJTBD / Next Move Theory. The report links here once at the top; you don't need to read it front to back.

- **Job** — what a person is really trying to get done: their situation now (State A) and the outcome they want (State B), in order to perform a higher-level Job. Not a feature, not a product — a unit of *motivation*. Stated as *"When {situation + trigger}, I want to {outcome} with {success criteria}, in order to {higher-level outcome}."*
- **Core Job** — the highest-level Job *your product performs fully*. Where the product lives.
- **Big Job** — the larger life or business outcome one level above the Core Job. It carries the customer's motivation; your product contributes to it but doesn't fully perform it.
- **Small Job** — a Job at the same level as your Core Job that your product *doesn't* do (a competitor, a partner, or the customer does it). The main source of growth ideas.
- **Micro Job** — a small step one level below the Core Job, where day-to-day experience lives.
- **Job Graph** — the map of how all these Jobs relate for a segment. Levels are relative to *your* product's reach, not fixed.
- **Success criteria** — the concrete, measurable marks by which the customer judges the outcome was reached "well enough" (e.g., "alert ≥2 hours out," "under $25"). The specification of value.
- **Value** — greater energy efficiency for the customer's brain in getting a Job done (better outcome for less time / money / effort / stress), measured against what their brain predicted.
- **Aha Moment** — the pleasant surprise when your product performs the Job *better than the customer expected* — the signal that value landed.
- **Problem** — the unpleasant surprise when a product performs *below* the customer's expected criteria. A Problem is always downstream of a Solution hired for a Job and underperforming — not a root cause.
- **Solution** — anything the customer uses to get a Job done (a product, a workaround, hiring someone, doing nothing). Switching solutions means switching the underlying set of Jobs.
- **Segment** — a group of people performing **similar Core Jobs with similar success criteria**. Not a demographic or an industry — those are at most secondary correlates.
- **Switchable demand** — the share of a segment that is *unsatisfied and willing to switch* (the Problem is the trigger). A big segment that is happy with its current solution is not a buyable market.
- **Map of Segments** — the set of distinct Job-based segments in a market, with their size, budget, and reachability, used to choose where to compete.
- **Selection screen** — the five tests this report scores each segment on: (1) noticeable added value, (2) demand we can generate, (3) target margin per paying customer, (4) size in money × switchability, (5) no existential (legal / impossible-tech) risk.
- **Consideration Activators** — the pieces of information that, when they reach the customer's head, move their choice toward you (a better way exists; it's better by these criteria; here's the product and the first step; your fears about it are handled; the alternative's problems are real).
- **Barrier** — an objective condition that stops a customer from using a new solution at all (a missing integration, an unsupported region, a compliance gap). Reality work, not messaging.
- **Critical Chain of Jobs** — the Job Graph laid out on a timeline: the actual sequence the customer walks, where the Aha Moment should fire and where things break.
- **Positioning** — the link between the Core Job you perform and the Big Job the customer cares about.
- **RAT (Riskiest Assumption Test)** — listing the beliefs the idea depends on (market → segment+Jobs → value → unit economics → channels), ranking them by how lethal they'd be if wrong and how cheap they are to test, and buying evidence against the worst ones first. The aim is to *kill or pivot* cheaply, not to confirm.
- **Pivot** — a change in the set of risky assumptions — often switching to a different Big-Job market that fits the same assets (technology, team, resources) better.
- **TAM / SAM / SOM** — Total / Serviceable / Obtainable market: the whole money pool for the Big Job, the slice you can serve in your country/segments, and the slice you can realistically capture in 1–2 years.
